Design Process

Problem

Users often cannot identify subtle posture errors during home Pilates sessions, resulting in reduced exercise quality and higher risk of strain without expert supervision.

Target Users

Primary users were beginner-to-intermediate Pilates practitioners training independently. Secondary users included coaches requiring objective movement and pressure data.

User Needs

Key needs were immediate correction cues, comfortable form factor, trustworthy alignment feedback, and a simple interface that does not interrupt exercise flow.

Design Requirements

Requirements included sub-50ms feedback, reliable Bluetooth communication, stable sensing under movement noise, and practical hardware constraints for Arduino-based prototyping.

IMUPressure SensorsBluetoothArduino

Ideation

Explored alternative sensing architectures and feedback strategies, comparing IMU-only, pressure-only, and sensor-fusion approaches to maximise posture detection reliability.

Feature Prioritisation

Prioritised core features first: IMU motion tracking, pressure sensing array, Bluetooth data transfer, and a real-time feedback interface before adding lower-impact enhancements.

Prototyping

Built iterative Arduino-based hardware prototypes, evaluating sensor placement, enclosure practicality, signal quality, and integration with C++ signal-processing pipelines.

Testing

Ran movement and posture scenarios to validate detection accuracy, latency, and false-positive behaviour. Sensor fusion achieved approximately 95% misalignment detection accuracy.

Iteration

Refined filtering, thresholds, and feedback timing to achieve responsive and stable guidance, with end-to-end response times under approximately 50ms in final testing.
